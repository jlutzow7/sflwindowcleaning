Gutter Cleaning

Protect Your Home From Water Damage

South Florida's rainy season is intense. Afternoon storms can drop several inches of rain in a matter of hours, and when gutters are clogged with leaves, seed pods, palm debris, and the fine sediment that accumulates over time, that water has nowhere to go. It backs up under the roofline, overflows down fascia and soffits, and pools against the foundation.

The damage from neglected gutters adds up quickly. Water that repeatedly saturates fascia boards causes wood rot. Overflow that runs down exterior walls encourages mold and mildew growth on stucco and siding. Water that pools at the foundation can work its way into crawl spaces and cause structural issues over time. In South Florida, where heavy rain is a regular part of life for nearly half the year, clean functioning gutters are not optional — they are essential.

We clean gutters thoroughly, removing all debris by hand and by flush, and verify that every downspout is clear and flowing properly before we leave. If we find any sagging sections, separation at the joints, or other issues during the cleaning, we will let you know so you can address them before they become a bigger problem.

What Our Gutter Cleaning Includes

  • Complete removal of leaves, debris, seed pods, and sediment
  • Downspout flushing and flow verification
  • Inspection for sagging sections, joint separation, or drainage issues
  • Any pre-existing conditions noted and reported to the homeowner
  • Residential homes, townhomes, and small commercial properties
  • Protects fascia, soffits, foundation, and landscaping
How often? We recommend gutter cleaning two to three times per year in South Florida — before rainy season in May, mid-season in August, and after peak leaf fall in December. Properties surrounded by mature trees or with heavy palm debris may benefit from quarterly service.
