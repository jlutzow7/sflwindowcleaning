Solar Panel Cleaning

Dirty Panels Are Costing You Money Every Day

Solar panels are a significant investment, and most homeowners and businesses install them expecting years of reliable energy production. But the performance of a solar array depends almost entirely on how much sunlight reaches the photovoltaic cells — and anything sitting on the surface of the glass blocks that sunlight.

In South Florida, panels face a particularly challenging environment. Salt air from the Atlantic and Gulf coasts leaves a fine mineral film on panel surfaces that builds up over time. Pollen season deposits thick layers of fine dust across entire arrays. Summer storm runoff leaves behind hard water minerals and sediment. Bird droppings create concentrated spots that block sunlight and can etch into panel glass if left for extended periods. And the intense subtropical sun bakes all of it onto the surface, making it harder to remove the longer it sits.

Rain alone is not enough. While rain can rinse loose dust from panels, it also carries minerals and deposits from the atmosphere that dry on the glass and leave residue behind. Panels that rely on rain cleaning typically underperform compared to professionally maintained arrays.

How Much Efficiency Are You Losing?

Research from the solar industry consistently shows that dirty panels can lose between 15 and 25 percent of their energy output, and in coastal or high-pollen environments the losses can be even greater. For a residential system, that loss translates directly into a higher electric bill. For a commercial array, it means reduced return on a substantial capital investment.

Regular professional cleaning restores that lost output and keeps your system performing the way it was designed to.

Our Method

Pure Water Cleaning — Safe, Effective, and Residue-Free

We use a pure water cleaning system for all solar panel cleaning. Ordinary tap water is run through a multi-stage filtration process that removes minerals, dissolved solids, and impurities before it ever contacts your panels. The purified water then actively draws contaminants off the panel surface and evaporates completely clean — leaving no soap film, no mineral deposits, and no residue of any kind.

We use soft, non-abrasive brushes specifically designed for solar panel surfaces. No harsh chemicals. No pressure washing. No abrasive pads or tools that could scratch or damage the glass coating on your panels.

Pre-Cleaning Inspection

Before we begin any solar panel cleaning, we conduct a thorough visual inspection of the entire array. We look for and document any pre-existing conditions including cracked panels, damaged frames, loose connections, compromised seals, or any other issues that were present before our arrival.

This protects you and it protects us. You receive an honest assessment of your system's condition before any cleaning takes place, and any issues we find are clearly documented so there is no question about what existed before our work began.

  • Pure water system — no chemicals, soaps, or detergents
  • Soft non-abrasive brushes safe for all panel surfaces
  • No pressure washing — gentle rinse only
  • Pre-cleaning inspection documents existing conditions
  • Cracked, damaged, or loose panels noted before work begins
  • Spot-free, residue-free finish on all panel glass
  • Safe for all major panel brands and mounting systems
Why pure water? Because it leaves absolutely nothing behind. When purified water evaporates, it takes everything it lifted off the panel with it — no spots, no film, no residue. Your panels dry crystal clear.
