Exterior Soft Washing

The Right Way to Clean a South Florida Home

South Florida's climate is hard on home exteriors. The combination of heat, humidity, salt air, and frequent rain creates ideal conditions for mold, mildew, and algae to grow on stucco walls, painted surfaces, soffits, and roof lines. What starts as a faint gray or green tint can spread quickly across an entire exterior if left untreated.

Many homeowners assume pressure washing is the answer — but pressure washing the wrong surfaces can cause serious damage. High pressure can crack or pit stucco, blast paint off walls, force water behind siding, damage caulking around windows and doors, and strip tiles and coatings loose from a roof. Once that damage is done it is expensive to repair.

Soft washing is a different approach entirely. Instead of using high pressure to blast away buildup, soft washing uses low-pressure water — similar to a garden hose — combined with environmentally safe cleaning solutions that break down and kill mold, mildew, algae, and organic growth at the source. The result is a thorough, lasting clean that is safe for your home's exterior surfaces and your roof.

Why South Florida Homes Need Soft Washing

Stucco is the dominant exterior finish on South Florida homes, and it is particularly vulnerable to organic growth. The porous texture of stucco traps moisture and provides an ideal surface for mold and algae to take hold. Salt air from the coast adds another layer of buildup that dulls the appearance of even freshly painted homes. Shaded areas, north-facing walls, and surfaces near landscaping tend to accumulate growth faster than sun-exposed areas.

Regular soft washing removes that buildup before it can stain permanently, protecting your paint, your stucco, and the overall appearance and value of your home.

Roof Cleaning

Soft Wash Roof Cleaning in South Florida

If your roof has developed dark streaks running down from the ridge, you're not looking at dirt — you're looking at Gloeocapsa magma, a bacteria-fed algae that thrives in Florida's heat and humidity and spreads across roofs of every material. It's the single most common reason South Florida homeowners search for roof cleaning, and it only gets worse the longer it's left alone.

A lot of homeowners hesitate to have their roof cleaned because they picture a pressure washer blasting away at their shingles or tiles — and that hesitation is well-founded. High-pressure roof cleaning can crack tiles, strip the granules off shingles, and force water up under the roofing material, causing leaks that show up months later. That's exactly why soft wash roof cleaning exists: it uses the same low-pressure, dwell-time method we use on stucco and siding, applied to your roof instead. The cleaning solution does the work, not the water pressure, so the algae and staining are killed at the root without any risk to the roofing material itself.

Because soft wash roof cleaning kills the algae rather than just knocking it loose from the surface, the results last significantly longer than a pressure wash would — and there's no risk of loosened tiles, cracked shingles, or voided roofing warranties that can come with high-pressure methods.

Roof Cleaning for Every Roofing Material

Soft washing is the correct method regardless of what your roof is made of — the technique adjusts to the material, not the other way around.

  • Tile roof cleaning — the most common roofing material in South Florida, highly susceptible to algae staining between the tile grooves
  • Shingle roof cleaning — removes black streaking without stripping protective granules the way pressure washing can
  • Metal roof cleaning — safely removes algae, pollen, and organic buildup from standing-seam and metal panel roofing without scratching the finish

Metal roofs are increasingly common on South Florida homes, and while they're more resistant to algae staining than tile, they're not immune — and pressure washing a metal roof risks denting panels and damaging seams. Soft washing cleans metal roofing safely and is also the correct method for cleaning around any solar panels mounted on the roof, since it won't dislodge fittings or scratch panel glass the way high-pressure equipment can.

How often? Most South Florida roofs benefit from soft wash roof cleaning every 2 to 3 years. Homes with heavy tree cover, north-facing slopes, or existing dark streaking may need attention sooner.

Our Method

We use environmentally safe, biodegradable cleaning solutions that are effective against mold, mildew, algae, and organic staining without harming your landscaping, plants, or surrounding surfaces. Soft Washing vs Pressure Washing

Knowing Which Method Is Right for Your Surface

One of the most common questions we get is whether a home needs soft washing or pressure washing. The answer depends entirely on the surface being cleaned.

Soft washing is the right choice for roofs, exterior walls, stucco, painted surfaces, siding, soffits, fascia, and any area where high pressure could cause damage or where organic growth needs to be chemically treated to be properly removed.

Pressure washing is the right choice for hard, durable surfaces like concrete driveways, brick pavers, pool decks, and sidewalks — surfaces that can handle high pressure and where chemical treatment is less important than mechanical removal of ground-in grime.

Many South Florida homes need both — soft washing for the roof and exterior walls and pressure washing for the driveway and pool deck. We can assess your property and recommend the right approach for each surface during a free on-site visit.
