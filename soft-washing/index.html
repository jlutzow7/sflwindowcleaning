Exterior Soft Washing

The Right Way to Clean a South Florida Home

South Florida's climate is hard on home exteriors. The combination of heat, humidity, salt air, and frequent rain creates ideal conditions for mold, mildew, and algae to grow on stucco walls, painted surfaces, soffits, and roof lines. What starts as a faint gray or green tint can spread quickly across an entire exterior if left untreated.

Many homeowners assume pressure washing is the answer — but pressure washing the wrong surfaces can cause serious damage. High pressure can crack or pit stucco, blast paint off walls, force water behind siding, damage caulking around windows and doors, and strip the protective coatings from painted surfaces. Once that damage is done it is expensive to repair.

Soft washing is a different approach entirely. Instead of using high pressure to blast away buildup, soft washing uses low-pressure water — similar to a garden hose — combined with environmentally safe cleaning solutions that break down and kill mold, mildew, algae, and organic growth at the source. The result is a thorough, lasting clean that is safe for your home's exterior surfaces.

Why South Florida Homes Need Soft Washing

Stucco is the dominant exterior finish on South Florida homes, and it is particularly vulnerable to organic growth. The porous texture of stucco traps moisture and provides an ideal surface for mold and algae to take hold. Salt air from the coast adds another layer of buildup that dulls the appearance of even freshly painted homes. Shaded areas, north-facing walls, and surfaces near landscaping tend to accumulate growth faster than sun-exposed areas.

Regular soft washing removes that buildup before it can stain permanently, protecting your paint, your stucco, and the overall appearance and value of your home.

Our Method

Low Pressure. Environmentally Safe Solutions. Lasting Results.

We use environmentally safe, biodegradable cleaning solutions that are effective against mold, mildew, algae, and organic staining without harming your landscaping, plants, or surrounding surfaces. Our solutions are applied at low pressure and allowed to dwell on the surface, breaking down organic growth at the root rather than just rinsing it away.

This is an important distinction. Pressure washing removes what you can see on the surface, but it does not kill the underlying mold or algae spores. Those spores remain on the surface and regrow quickly. Soft washing with the right cleaning solution kills the growth at the source, which is why soft washed surfaces stay cleaner significantly longer than pressure washed ones.

Surfaces We Soft Wash

Soft washing is the appropriate cleaning method for any exterior surface that could be damaged by high pressure or that requires a chemical treatment to properly remove organic growth.

  • Stucco exterior walls — the most common South Florida finish

  • Vinyl siding and soffit panels
  • Fascia boards and eave areas

How often? Most South Florida home exteriors benefit from soft washing every 12 to 18 months. Homes near the water, backing to preserves, or with heavy shade tend to accumulate growth faster and may need more frequent attention.

Soft Washing vs Pressure Washing

Knowing Which Method Is Right for Your Surface

One of the most common questions we get is whether a home needs soft washing or pressure washing. The answer depends entirely on the surface being cleaned.

Soft washing is the right choice for exterior walls, stucco, painted surfaces, siding, soffits, fascia, and any area where high pressure could cause damage or where organic growth needs to be chemically treated to be properly removed.

Pressure washing is the right choice for hard, durable surfaces like concrete driveways, brick pavers, pool decks, and sidewalks — surfaces that can handle high pressure and where chemical treatment is less important than mechanical removal of ground-in grime.

Many South Florida homes need both — soft washing for the exterior walls and pressure washing for the driveway and pool deck. We can assess your property and recommend the right approach for each surface during a free on-site visit.
