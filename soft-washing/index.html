Exterior Soft Washing

The Right Way to Clean a South Florida Home

South Florida's climate is tough on home exteriors. The combination of heat, humidity, salt air, and frequent rain creates ideal conditions for mold, mildew, and algae to grow on stucco walls, painted surfaces, soffits, and rooflines. What starts as a light gray or green tint can spread across an entire exterior quickly if left untreated.

Many homeowners assume pressure washing is the answer, but pressure washing the wrong surfaces can cause serious damage. High pressure can crack or pit stucco, strip paint off walls, force water behind siding, damage caulking around windows and doors, and dislodge tiles and shingles from a roof. Once that damage is done, it's costly to repair.

Soft washing is a completely different approach. Instead of using high pressure to blast away buildup, soft washing uses low-pressure water — similar to a garden hose — combined with environmentally-safe cleaning solutions that break down and kill mold, mildew, algae, and organic growth at the source. The result is a deep, long-lasting clean that's safe for your home's exterior surfaces and for your roof.

Why South Florida Homes Need Soft Washing

Stucco is the dominant exterior finish for South Florida homes, and it's particularly vulnerable to organic growth. Stucco's porous texture traps moisture and gives mold and algae an ideal surface to take hold. Salt air from the coast adds another layer of grime that dulls the appearance even of freshly painted homes. Shaded areas, north-facing walls, and surfaces near landscaping tend to build up growth faster than sun-exposed areas. Homeowners searching for house soft washing near me in Fort Lauderdale, Davie, or Cooper City are usually dealing with exactly this kind of stucco staining.

Regular soft washing removes that buildup before it permanently stains, protecting your paint, your stucco, and the overall appearance and value of your home.

Roof Cleaning

Roof Cleaning With Soft Washing in South Florida

If your roof has developed dark streaks running down from the ridge line, you're not looking at dirt — you're looking at Gloeocapsa magma, a bacteria-fed algae that thrives in Florida's heat and humidity and spreads across roofs of all material types. It's the most common reason South Florida homeowners search for roof cleaning near me, and it only gets worse the longer it's left untreated.

Many homeowners hesitate to have their roof cleaned because they picture a pressure washer blasting away their shingles or tiles — and that hesitation is well founded. High-pressure roof cleaning can crack tiles, strip shingle granules, and force water beneath roofing material, causing leaks that show up months later. This is exactly why roof soft washing exists: it uses the same low-pressure, dwell-time method we use on stucco and siding, applied to your roof. The cleaning solution does the work, not water pressure, so algae and staining are killed at the root without any risk to the roofing material itself.

Because roof soft washing kills algae rather than just dislodging it from the surface, results last significantly longer than a pressure wash — and there's no risk of loose tiles, cracked shingles, or voided roof warranties like there can be with high-pressure methods. We offer roof algae removal in Fort Lauderdale, Davie, Cooper City, and throughout Broward, Palm Beach, and Miami-Dade Counties.

Roof Cleaning for Every Material Type

Soft washing is the right method no matter what your roof is made of — the technique adapts to the material, not the other way around.

  • Tile roof cleaning — the most common roofing material in South Florida, highly susceptible to algae staining between tile grooves
  • Shingle roof cleaning — removes black streaking without stripping protective granules the way pressure washing can
  • Metal roof cleaning — safely removes algae, pollen, and organic buildup from standing seam and panel metal roofs without scratching the finish

Metal roofs are becoming more common on South Florida homes, and while they're more resistant to algae staining than tile, they're not immune — and pressure washing a metal roof risks denting panels and damaging seams. Soft washing cleans the metal roof safely and is also the right method for cleaning around any roof-mounted solar panels, since it won't dislodge mounting hardware or scratch panel glass the way high-pressure equipment can.

How often? Most South Florida roofs benefit from soft washing every 2-3 years. Homes with heavy tree cover, north-facing slopes, or existing dark streaking may need attention sooner.

Soft Washing vs Pressure Washing

Know Which Method Is Right for Your Surface

One of the most common questions we get from homeowners in Fort Lauderdale, Davie, and Cooper City is whether their home needs soft washing or pressure washing. The answer depends entirely on the surface being cleaned.

Soft washing is the right choice for roofs, exterior walls, stucco, painted surfaces, siding, soffits, fascia boards, and any area where high pressure could cause damage or where organic growth needs to be chemically treated to be properly removed.

Pressure washing is the right choice for hard, durable surfaces like concrete driveways, brick pavers, pool decks, and sidewalks — surfaces that can withstand high pressure and where chemical treatment is less important than mechanical removal of ground-in dirt.

Many South Florida homes need both — soft washing for the roof and exterior walls, and pressure washing for the driveway and pool deck. We can assess your property and recommend the right approach for each surface during a free on-site visit.

FAQ

Soft Washing & Roof Cleaning Questions, Answered

How often should I soft wash my roof in South Florida?

Most South Florida roofs benefit from soft washing every 2 to 3 years. Homes with heavy tree cover, north-facing slopes, or existing dark algae streaking may need attention sooner.

What's the difference between soft washing and pressure washing?

Soft washing uses low-pressure water combined with cleaning solutions that kill mold, mildew, and algae at the root, making it safe for roofs, stucco, and painted surfaces. Pressure washing uses high pressure and is better suited to hard, durable surfaces like concrete driveways and pavers that can withstand mechanical force without damage.

Is soft washing safe for tile, shingle, and metal roofs?

Yes. Soft washing adapts to the roof material rather than the other way around, making it safe for tile, shingle, and metal roofs. It removes algae staining and grime without stripping shingle granules, cracking tiles, or scratching metal panel finishes.

Why does my roof have black streaks?

Black streaks on a South Florida roof are almost always caused by Gloeocapsa magma, a bacteria-fed algae that thrives in Florida's heat and humidity. It spreads across roofs of all material types and gets worse the longer it's left untreated.

How often should I soft wash my home's exterior?

Most South Florida home exteriors benefit from soft washing every 12 to 18 months. Homes near water, backing to preserves, or in shaded areas tend to build up mold and algae faster and may need more frequent cleaning.

Is soft washing safe for landscaping and plants?

Yes. We use biodegradable, environmentally-safe cleaning solutions that are effective against mold, mildew, and algae without harming landscaping, plants, or surrounding surfaces.
