Our Method

The Pure Water Difference

Most window cleaners use soap — which sounds clean, but soap leaves an invisible residue that attracts dust and causes streaks. We use a different approach entirely.

Our multi-stage filtration system removes 99.9% of minerals and impurities from the water. Because pure water is "hungry" for contaminants, it lifts and rinses away dirt completely — and when it evaporates, it leaves absolutely nothing behind.

  • Zero chemicals — safe around kids, pets, and landscaping
  • Stays cleaner longer with no soap residue to attract dust
  • Reaches upper floors safely from the ground using water-fed poles
  • Flawless, streak-free finish every single time
01

Multi-Stage Filtration

Tap water passes through sediment, carbon, and reverse osmosis filters to remove chlorine, minerals, and dissolved solids.

02

Deionization (DI) Stage

A final DI resin stage brings the water to 000 TDS — completely pure, with no dissolved content left to leave spots.

03

Water-Fed Pole Application

Pure water is delivered through a soft brush on an extendable pole, agitating and rinsing the glass simultaneously — no ladders needed.

04

Spot-Free Drying

Because the water contains nothing, it evaporates completely clean — leaving a flawless, sparkling finish with zero residue.

Solar Panel Cleaning

Dirty Panels Cost You Money Every Day

Studies show that dirty solar panels can lose 15–25% of their energy output — sometimes more in coastal environments like South Florida. Salt deposits, pollen, bird droppings, and baked-on grime all block the sunlight your panels need to perform.

Rain does not clean your panels. It moves dirt around and leaves behind mineral deposits as it evaporates. Professional cleaning with purified water is the only way to restore full efficiency.

  • Restores lost energy output — often immediately measurable
  • Pure water leaves zero mineral residue on panel glass
  • Non-abrasive soft brushes — manufacturer safe
  • Residential rooftop arrays and commercial ground mounts
  • Safe for all major panel brands
How often? We recommend every 3–4 months for most South Florida properties. Coastal homes within a mile of the ocean often benefit from every 6–8 weeks due to salt spray accumulation.

Exterior Soft Washing

The Right Way to Clean Stucco & Painted Surfaces

Pressure washing is powerful — but point it at stucco, painted siding, or delicate trim and you risk blasting paint off, forcing water behind your siding, and etching surfaces permanently.

Soft washing uses low-pressure water — similar to a garden hose — combined with professional-grade biodegradable cleaning solutions that kill mold, mildew, and algae at the root.

  • Safe for stucco, Hardie board, vinyl, and painted wood
  • Kills mold and algae — doesn't just rinse it away
  • Protects paint, caulk, and architectural details
  • Results last 4–6x longer than pressure washing alone
  • Biodegradable solutions — safe for plants and landscaping
How often? Most South Florida home exteriors benefit from soft washing every 12–18 months. Homes near the water, backing to preserves, or in shaded areas may need attention more frequently.

Gutter Cleaning

Protect Your Home From Water Damage

South Florida's rainy season brings intense downpours. When gutters are clogged with leaves, seed pods, palm debris, and fine sediment, that water has nowhere to go — it backs up under your roofline and pools against your foundation.

We clear your gutters completely, flush the downspouts, and make sure everything is flowing properly before we leave.

  • Complete removal of leaves, debris, and sediment buildup
  • Downspout flushing and flow verification
  • Inspection for sagging sections or drainage issues
  • Residential homes, townhomes, and small commercial
  • Protects fascia, soffits, foundation, and landscaping
How often? We recommend gutter cleaning 2–3 times per year — before rainy season (May), mid-season (August), and after peak leaf fall (December).
